I. I've been stopped by the police in public :

You have the right to remain silent.You do not have to consent to a search of yourself or your belongings, but police may pat down your clothing if they suspect a weapon. If you are arrested by police, you have the right to a government-appointed lawyer if you cannot afford one.

II. I've been pulled over by the police :

Both drivers and passengers have the right to remain silent.If you're a passenger, you can ask if you're free to leave. If yes, you may silently leave.

III. The police are at my door :

You should not invite the officer into your house.Ask the officer to slip the warrant under the door or hold it up to the window so you can read it.Even if officers have a warrant, you have the right to remain silent.

IV. I've been arrested by the police :

Say you wish to remain silent and ask for a lawyer immediately. You have the right to make a local phone call.The police cannot listen if you call a lawyer.

V. The police violated my rights :

When you can, write down everything you remember, including the officers' badge and patrol car numbers and the agency they work for. Get contact information for witnesses. If you're injured, seek medical attention immediately and take photographs of your injuries. File a written complaint with the agency's internal affairs division or civilian complaint board.
